THE BOTTOM LINE
Spotify's ASO health is inadequate, especially against a notable competitor like YouTube. The current strategy lacks depth in keyword targeting, visibility, and engagement potential. It requires urgent changes to capture market share effectively.

KEYWORD STRATEGY
What's working:

Spotify includes general music-related keywords that draw some traffic, like "music" and "songs," capturing both casual and serious music listeners.
The app mostly retains a high relevance score due to consistent user engagement metrics, such as time spent in-app and daily active users.
What's broken:

The app title lacks essential keywords that could enhance visibility; "Spotify" alone does not convey enough about its service offering.
There's a lack of long-tail keywords, which limits the app's reach; targeting terms like "stream music" or "music playlists" is virtually absent.
Missed opportunity for localized keyword optimization has resulted in lower visibility in non-English speaking regions, where phrases like "musique" or "canciones" could be valuable.

TITLE & METADATA
Current title problems:

The current title lacks key information about the app’s primary functionalities and features, limiting discoverability. It could also benefit from being more engaging; currently, it's mostly just the brand name "Spotify."
Rewritten title suggestion:

"Spotify: Stream Your Favorite Music & Playlists" — Clearly conveys functionality and encourages exploration.
"Spotify: Ultimate Music Experience & Personalized Playlists" — Highlights user benefits and engages listeners.
"Spotify: Discover New Music & Create Playlists" — Directly addresses user needs, enhancing appeal.
Metadata Score: 5/10

DESCRIPTION AUDIT
First 3 lines (most critical):
The current first three lines do not provide concrete benefits and are not engaging. Instead, they sound generic and fail to entice the user, missing an opportunity to convey immediate value.

Full description problems:

The whole description is not optimized for user engagement; it’s more of a list rather than a compelling story.
There’s a lack of urgency or a call-to-action that could push users to download the app immediately.
Rewritten opening paragraph:

Description Score: 4/10

COMPETITOR ANALYSIS vs YouTube
YouTube has several advantages over Spotify, especially in its diverse multimedia format. While Spotify focuses solely on audio, YouTube offers music videos, user-generated content, and a strong social sharing component, making it a more engaging platform for many users. YouTube’s playlist features also allow for more expansive content sorting and discovery, drawing users into an ecosystem that successfully retains attention. Spotify could improve its exploration capabilities to provide unique personalized playlists that not only mirror listening habits but also proactively suggest music based on trends and community listening habits. Additionally, YouTube’s vibrant community interactions through comments and likes create a user engagement level that Spotify lacks. Focus on enhancing social features could yield dividends.

RATINGS & REVIEWS HEALTH
Spotify generally enjoys good ratings, often averaging around 4.5 stars. The primary complaints include frequent bugs during updates and issues with offline downloads. However, users particularly appreciate the vast music library and user interface, indicating that while the backend has faults, the essential offering delivers satisfaction.
